Our Mission

We Built This Because the Signal-to-Noise Ratio Was Terrible

"We built CryptoLens because we were tired of noise. Most crypto content is either pure hype or surface-level price commentary. We wanted something deeper — analysis grounded in data, not Discord rumors."

CryptoLens aggregates real-time news from the top crypto media outlets and layers independent market analysis on top of it. We don't take coin sponsorships, we don't run paid promotions, and we don't have referral links embedded in our reports. Our only incentive is to be useful to you.

Our market data comes directly from CoinGecko — one of the most widely trusted independent aggregators in the industry, tracking over 10,000 coins across hundreds of exchanges. We supplement their price data with publicly available on-chain analytics and macro market indicators to build the reports and consulting frameworks we offer.

Methodology

Where Our Data Comes From

We're transparent about our sources. If we can't cite it, we don't publish it.

  • CoinGecko API

    Primary source for all price data, market cap rankings, trading volume, on-chain supply data, trending coins, and the news feed displayed on this site. CoinGecko aggregates data from 900+ exchanges and is widely regarded as one of the most comprehensive independent crypto data providers.

  • On-Chain Analytics (Glassnode, Dune Analytics)

    For report research — metrics like MVRV (Market Value to Realized Value), SOPR (Spent Output Profit Ratio), NVT ratio, exchange net flow, and miner outflows. These indicators are referenced in our reports and cited explicitly.

  • Macro Overlay (DXY, Fed Rate Data, Global M2)

    Crypto markets are increasingly correlated with macro conditions. Our cycle reports incorporate DXY (US Dollar Index), Federal Reserve rate decisions, and global M2 money supply trends to contextualize crypto price action.

  • Technical Analysis (Weekly Close Data)

    Support and resistance levels in our reports are drawn from weekly closing prices — not intraday noise. We do not publish day-trading signals or short-term price targets.
